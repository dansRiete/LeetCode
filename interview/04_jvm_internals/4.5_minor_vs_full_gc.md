# 4.5 — Minor GC vs Full GC

What is the difference between Minor GC and Full GC? Which is more dangerous and why?

## Complete Answer

### Minor GC:
- Scans only **Young Generation** (small, ~10-20% of heap)
- Fast — typically <10ms
- Triggered when Eden space is full
- Surviving objects promoted to Survivor space or Old Gen

### Full GC:
- Scans **entire heap** including Old Generation (can be GBs)
- Also performs **compaction** — moves surviving objects together to eliminate fragmentation
- Slow — can be seconds of STW pause
- Dangerous in production: all threads stopped for duration

### What triggers Full GC:
1. Old Generation is full — no room to promote from Young Gen
2. `System.gc()` called explicitly — avoid in production
3. Metaspace full — class metadata exhausted (dynamic class generation)

### Rule of thumb:
- Tune to keep most objects dying in Young Gen (short-lived)
- Minimize promotion to Old Gen to avoid Full GC
- If Full GCs are frequent → investigate memory leaks or unbounded caches
